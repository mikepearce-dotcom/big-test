Selected engagements

Three examples of how the work moves from diagnosis into redesign and commercial change.

The sectors are different, but the logic stays consistent: identify where confidence or clarity breaks down, redesign the page or journey around the real decision, and make the next action easier to take.

P&O Ferries

Redesigning the booking flow after an audit.

Travel Booking flow

Commercial challenge

The booking journey was leaking value before final commitment, even though intent and demand were already there.

What the audit surfaced

  • Key steps in the flow were creating hesitation before commitment
  • Important reassurance did not arrive early enough in the journey
  • Progression through the booking path was carrying too much friction

What changed

  • The booking flow was redesigned around clearer step logic
  • Decision anxiety was reduced with stronger reassurance at the right moments
  • The path into commitment became more legible and easier to follow
Outcome £1.1m incremental revenue

An audit-first engagement that turned diagnosed friction into a redesigned journey with measurable commercial upside.

Samsung

Redesigning the phone page product card to improve add to basket.

Retail Phone PDP

Commercial challenge

The product page had demand, but the product card was not doing enough to convert consideration into basket action.

What diagnosis surfaced

  • Core purchase information and reassurance were competing instead of reinforcing one another
  • The card hierarchy was not helping users move cleanly from interest to action
  • The add-to-basket moment needed more clarity and confidence built into it

What changed

  • The phone page product card was redesigned around stronger hierarchy
  • Decision support and reassurance were brought closer to the basket action
  • The final action was made easier to recognise and commit to
Outcome +16% add to basket

A focused page-level intervention that improved commercial behaviour without needing a whole-site redesign.

Black Piano

A broader website redesign to sharpen positioning and make the offer feel premium.

B2B services Website redesign

Commercial challenge

The site needed to look more credible, feel more coherent, and do a better job of carrying visitors into a sales conversation.

What diagnosis surfaced

  • The proposition needed to feel clearer and more immediate
  • The page structure was not doing enough to support trust and progression
  • The overall presentation needed to better match the quality of the service

What changed

  • The website was redesigned around a cleaner premium presentation
  • Service pathways were made easier to understand and act on
  • The enquiry route was made more structured and commercially focused
Outcome Full website redesign with a stronger premium proposition.

Not every meaningful redesign has a public number, but the commercial role of the work is still clear in how the site sells.

How proof gets stronger

The best public case studies pair exact numbers with clear intervention detail.

  • When a figure is public, anchor it to the specific journey or component that changed
  • When the work is broader, explain what the redesign was trying to fix commercially
  • Diagnosis matters because it explains why the redesign was the right one
